Royal Duchies

In Sweden, ducal provinces, called Duchies, have been allocated by the monarch to powerful Swedes since the 13th century. The ruler of a duchie is titled Duke or Duchess, and were historically the sons of kings and their wives. Traditionally, the Duke would rule over the province while remaining under the power of the king, thus helping the king extend his rule over the entire kingdom. The relationship between the king and a duke is comporable to that of the President of the Unites States and the Govenor of Virginia.

Today, members of the Royal family, including the children and grandchildren, are alotted a duchie at birth. However, much like other titles in the monarchy, it is more of a sign of importance than an actual power. Dukes and duchesses have little-to-no actual power their territory.

There are 26 duchies in Sweden that currrently have or historically have had ducal appointments. Additionally, there are a few provinces that have never had dukes.
